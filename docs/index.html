Glossary & Definitions

General Terms

EFC (Expected Family Contribution)

While you might not see this term on your award letter, you will see it on your FAFSA or DREAM Application confirmation page, known as a Student Aid Report (SAR). This is the dollar amount that the government is estimating that your family can contribute towards your educational and personal expenses for the academic year. It includes all costs itemized under "Cost of Attendance" above.

Cost of Attendance

The cost of attendance is the estimation of how much funding you'll need to cover all your educational and personal costs over the course of one Academic Year. Cost of attendance varies depending on your chosen institution, your living situation, etc. The calculation tends to be fairly accurate, however you may be able to save on a variety of areas under "Living Expenses" by using resources provided to you by your college, such as a bus pass, food pantries, etc.

Loans and Work Study

Subsidized Loans

Subsidized loans are loans where the federal government is paying your student loan interest while you are in school, and for up to 6 months after you drop below 6 units. Current interest for 2026 is 6.5%, and universities offer subsidized loans of up to $5,500 per year to students with needs. Subsidized loans are only for undergraduate students but you can continue to pay them off and get the benefits through your pay off date (usually 10 years).

Remember 6-6-6: ~6% interest starts 6 months after having less than 6 units.

(1) If you go directly to graduate school after completing your Bachelor's degree, with a break of less than 6 months, and you enroll in at least 6 units in graduate school (or even at Mission College), you won't accrue interest.
However, (2) if you take a break from school for longer than 6 months, or if you drop below 6 units for longer than 6 months, you will be in charge of paying interest, and the payments will begin. Subsidized loans are good loans, and better to use than a credit cards. They do go onto your credit report, so if after 6 months post graduation, you are unable to pay, please call them asap before it goes onto your credit report (within 30 days--sooner if you can).

Unsubsidized Loans

Unsubsidized loans begin accruing interest from the day you accept the loan.

How is interest accrued? There are many apps online that can break this down for you. Typically, your loans are calculated with a 10 year pay back period, but you can work with the student loans office (e.g. Nelnet) to negotiate terms such as repayment options.

Here is a sample of a loan calculator that you can use:
https://studentaid.gov/loan-simulator/

Work Study

Federal Work-Study provides part-time jobs for undergraduate and graduate students with financial need, allowing them to earn money to help pay education expenses (studentaid.gov). To take advantage of work study, you would need to apply for jobs on campus. Work study students have an advantage of getting a number of campus jobs because their wages are paid for by the federal government.
